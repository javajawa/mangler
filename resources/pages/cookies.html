Cookie Policy

This site uses only one cookie — a session cookie, named blog which will be deleted by your browser when it considers the session terminated. Consult your browser's documentation for more information.

No personal information is stored in this cookie. Basic session information is stored — the IP address, time of access, and any referer [sic] information that is supplied by your browser. Each request is logged with this information and the session id made the request.

You can opt of this tracking by disabling this sites cookies in your browser. This should not affect your viewing of the site. However, it will make it harder to tell post's popularity, making it harder to improve content.

You can find out more about cookies all around the web, or read the IETF's RFC 6265. The referer field as part of the HTTP, the standard protocol which allows browsers and web sites to communicate; it is defined in RFC 1945.

The requirement to post a cookie policy is partially implied by the EU e-Privacy Directive 2003 (2002/58/EC). Neither implied nor formal consent is needed to place cookies for essential tracking — such as a session cookie for maintaining a shopping basket. The session cookies here are not required; however, the only reliable way to perform a consent check also involves cookies.

The directive requires that consent is based on “specific and informed” consent. Continuing to browse this site having read this page will be taken as implied informed consent.
